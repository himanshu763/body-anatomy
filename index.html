// 01.A
Reality Check Honest assessment

At 93 kg on a 5'8" frame, joint load is significant — and it's not symmetric. The right BKA forces the left side of the kinetic chain to absorb compensatory loading: left ankle, left knee, left hip, and the right hip socket all take more than their share. The left shoulder typically rides high as a counterweight to balance gait.

The mission, in order: (1) strip body fat to reduce joint load — that's a nutrition problem, not a training one. (2) Build a posterior-chain engine that fixes the lateral lean. (3) Eliminate every exercise that asks the residual limb or compromised side to absorb impact.

// 01.B
Operating Principles Non-negotiable

PRINCIPLE

Stability First

Every upper-body lift uses a chest support, bench, or machine. Standing free-weight rows or overhead presses transfer rotational load to a foundation that can't accept it.

RULE 01 // SUPPORTED ONLY
PRINCIPLE

Pull > Push

2:1 ratio of pulling to pushing. Counteracts the forward slump that always follows lateral compensation, opens the chest, and pulls the head back over the spine.

RULE 02 // 2X PULL VOLUME
PRINCIPLE

Hip Hinge Sacred

The hip hinge — not the squat — is the lift that rebuilds glute strength without grinding the right socket. Cable pull-throughs and back extensions before any deadlift variation.

RULE 03 // HINGE BEFORE LOAD
PRINCIPLE

Diet Dictates Gravity

Caloric deficit is biomechanical, not cosmetic. Every kilo down reduces ~4kg of force through the left ankle at heel strike. Eat for the joints, not the mirror.

module 03 // pre-training prep

Rehab Prep

A 10-minute circuit. Done BEFORE you touch a weight. Three phases — release, activate, integrate — designed to unlock the chronic compensations from prosthetic gait so the working set isn't reinforcing them.

// PHASE 01
Release ~3 min

UPPER

Left Pec Minor Doorway

Forearm against doorframe at 90°. Rotate sternum to the right until you feel the stretch under the collarbone. The left pec minor pulls the left shoulder forward to balance you — release it.

2 × 60 SEC
NERVE

Left Sciatic Floss

Seated, slump posture. Straighten left leg + point toes down → bend knee + pull toes up. Pump continuously. Never hold the stretch — this is dynamic.

15 PUMPS × 2
HIP

Right Hip 90/90

Seated 90/90 with the right (residual) leg forward. Rotate gently between internal and external. The right socket loses ROM fast — this preserves it.

10 SLOW × 2 SIDES

// PHASE 02
Activate ~4 min

GLUTE

Right Side Clamshells

Lie on left side. Heels together, lift right knee. The right glute medius has likely been dormant for years — this wakes it. Slow eccentric.

2 × 15 (SLOW)
SCAP

Scapular Push-Up

Plank position, elbows locked. Sink shoulder blades, then push the floor away aggressively. Re-teaches scapular protraction without involving the elbow.

2 × 12
CORE

Dead Bug (Anti-Extension)

On back, arms up, knees bent at 90°. Lower opposite arm + leg while pressing low back into the floor. Don't let the ribcage flare. Core that resists rotation.

2 × 8 / SIDE

// PHASE 03
Integrate ~3 min

FINISHER

Wall-Supported Calf March

Both hands on wall. Slow left/right alternation with intentional foot pressure. Re-grooves a symmetric pattern before walking into the gym floor.

60 SEC
FINISHER

Cat-Cow Flow

10 slow reps. Decouples the thoracic spine from the lumbar — your lateral lean lives at the thoracolumbar junction. Breathe in extension, out in flexion.

10 SLOW REPS

module 05 // doctrine

The Iron Rules

Violate these and the compensations come back. The whole protocol is built around them.

  • 01
    Chest-Supported Only

    Because your lower-body foundation is asymmetrical, any standing overhead press or bent-over row forces the lower back to twist around the imbalance. Always use a bench, machine, or chest pad for upper-body work. The spine doesn't get to stabilize what the legs can't.

  • 02
    The 2:1 Pull Ratio

    For every pushing set (chest, front delts), you do two pulling sets (back, rear delts, rhomboids). Non-negotiable. This drags your shoulders back into position and fights the forward slump that prosthetic gait quietly drills into you.

  • 03
    Trap Stop Protocol

    If you're doing lateral raises or rows and your upper traps fire before the target muscle does — stop. Drop the weight by 30%. Reset. Your left trap is already overworked from carrying the counterbalance during walking; we are NOT training it harder.

  • 04
    Diet Dictates Gravity

    At 93kg every step sends force through the left foot and right socket equal to roughly 1.5× bodyweight. Dropping to 82kg drops that force by ~16kg per step, every step, all day. The caloric deficit is a biomechanical intervention. You cannot out-train a bad diet — and even if you could, your joints can't.

  • 05
    Sleep is Sets & Reps

    Less than 7 hours and the next session is a write-off — cortisol up, recovery down, hunger up. The residual limb interface heals during deep sleep. Treat 7.5h non-negotiable like you treat the workout itself.
