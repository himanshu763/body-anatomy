Biomechanical Assessment & Corrective Protocol

Custom Rehabilitation Strategy for Right BKA Compensations

1. Executive Summary

Your body is operating in a chronic, highly adapted state to accommodate the right below-knee amputation (BKA). Through myofascial slings, the lack of right ankle plantarflexion has created a lateral trunk lean, causing your left shoulder to act as a stabilizing counterweight. These are not isolated injuries, but a logical chain of kinetic compensations developed over 16 years.

2. Deep Anatomical Mapping

The Right Lower Quadrant (Prosthetic Side)

  • Muscular Amnesia: Weakened Gluteus Medius leading to pelvic drop.
  • Hip Flexor Contracture: Over-reliance on Iliopsoas/Rectus Femoris to "clear" the prosthetic, locking the hip in chronic flexion.
  • Myofascial Strain: Deconditioned stabilizers unable to handle isolated loads (QL/Glute Med).

The Left Lower Quadrant (Overworked Anchor)

  • Arch Collapse: Posterior Tibialis fatigue causing "flexible flat foot" under load.
  • Sciatic Nerve Entrapment: Rigid muscle tension compressing the S1 pathway, causing toe lift difficulty and numbness.

The Left Upper Quadrant (The Counterweight)

  • Scapular Dyskinesis: Winging caused by inhibited Serratus Anterior.
  • Trap Dominance: Upper Trapezius over-activity to stabilize a "floating" shoulder blade.
  • Anterior Impingement: Pectoralis Minor pulling the joint into internal rotation, causing clicking/blocking.

3. Corrective Exercise Protocol

Perform this 15-minute routine 4-5 times per week as a warm-up.

Phase 1: Tissue Release & Nerve Gliding

1. Left Pec Minor Doorway Stretch

Place left forearm against doorframe (elbow 2-3" above shoulder). Step through and rotate sternum right.

Prescription: 2 sets × 60 seconds.
2. Left Upper Trap Trigger Point Release

Use a lacrosse ball against a wall on tender knots. Tilt left ear toward right shoulder while applying pressure.

Prescription: 90 seconds of continuous pressure.
3. Sciatic Nerve Flossing (Left Leg)

Slumped sitting position. Straighten leg + point toes away; bend knee + pull toes up. Never hold a nerve stretch.

Prescription: 15-20 slow, continuous pumps.

Phase 2: Neurological Activation

1. Side-Lying Clamshells (Right Hip)

Lie on left side. Lift right knee while keeping heels together. Avoid rolling the lower back.

Prescription: 2 sets × 15 slow reps (3s eccentric).
2. Strict Scapular Push-Ups

Plank position, elbows locked. Squeeze blades together, then push floor away to spread them wide.

Prescription: 2 sets × 12 reps.
3. Isometric Seated Calf Holds (Left)

Sit with weighted thigh. Raise heel and hold the top position perfectly still to build tendon stiffness.

Prescription: 3 sets × 20-second holds.

Phase 3: Total Body Integration

The Bird-Dog

Quadruped position. Extend right leg and left arm simultaneously. Focus on glute and shoulder blade stability.

Prescription: 2 sets × 10 reps (3s hold).

4. Gym Rules & Modifications

The "Trap Stop"
End sets immediately if the upper trap/neck tenses to assist the movement.
Chest-Supported Only
Use an incline bench for all shoulder work to block torso swaying.
2:1 Pull-to-Push
Perform two sets of back/posterior work for every one set of chest/pressing.
Unilateral Focus
Swap Barbell Squats for Bulgarian Split Squats and Single-Leg Presses.

5. Daily Habits

  • Standing Awareness: Consciously shift weight back to center; stop leaning 100% on the left leg.
  • Shoulder Reset: Every 2 hours, roll the left shoulder up, back, and down into the "back pocket."
  • Sleep Hygiene: Sleep on back or right side. Avoid left-side sleeping to prevent joint crushing.

6. Healing Timeline

Weeks 1–3: Neuromuscular adaptation. Immediate reduction in neck tightness and clicking.
Weeks 4–8: Structural endurance. Left scapula sits flatter; right glute improves gait stability.
Months 3–6+: Tissue remodeling. Chest/hip resting lengths reset; visual symmetry improves.
